White spaghetti with peppered sauce stew

This delicacy is a popular meal eaten by many Nigerians, due to it's affordable and easy cooking process. It takes not much time to prepare and it leaves you with great, savoury taste.

Recipe

  1. 1 pack spaghetti
  2. Fresh pepper and tomatoes
  3. Maggi, salt,vegetable oil, Onion
  4. Meat

Procedure

Step 1:
Put spaghetti on fire, allow to almost cooked with lots of water. After which you filter and pour back without water into the pot. Allow to dry a little a d bring down.
Step 2:
Prepare your source by cutting the onion and slicing tomatoes and pepper.
Step 3:
Heat up the pot and and put a little veg oil,put onion and tomatoes, stir for 3mins,add salt,maggi and curry,strip 2mins. Straight up, foody is ready

Turmeric Porridge Beans and Rice

For beans lovers, this meal is one you would not want to miss. Tumeric porridge beans and rice is a popular option among Nigerians as it is very economical to prepare

Recipe

  1. Beans, Rice
  2. Palm Oil, Crushed Scotch Bonnet, Grated Turmeric
  3. Curry Powder, Thyme, Seasoning Cubes, Cinnamon Powder
  4. Salt, Diced Onions, Water
  5. Black Pepper Powder

Procedure

Step 1:
Rinse beans,drain and cook with 4Cups of water,wash rice with salt,rinse and drain.
Step 2:
Once it boils,add Onions to soften it fast.
Step 3:
After 5mns,add rice,stir from top with a wooden spatula.
Step 4:
You may top up with extra water,such that water covers both.
Step 5:
Once it boils,add ingredients except oil and salt. Step 6:
Top up with little more water till both are soft, before water completely dries off,taste to adjust with little salt or any ingredient of choice.
Step 7:
Add Palm oil when water is 97% dry,stir with a wooden spatula,cook for 2mins, enjoy.

Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup

Everybody in Nigeria seems to love pounded yam and Egusi. This meal is made mostly on special occasions and it ranks high on our list of equisite meals in Nigeria.

Recipe

  1. Yam
  2. Grinded Melon, Grinded Crayfish
  3. Vegetable Leaves
  4. Grated scotch and red bell peppers
  5. Minced garlic, onion
  6. Meat and meat stock
  7. Palm oil, seasoning cube and salt

Procedure

Step 1:
Peel the skins off the yam and slice. Rinse and place the yam in a pot and add enough water to the level of the yam.
Step 2:
Cover and cook the yam until they're fork tender. Put the boiled yam inside your Mortar and pound it with the pestle.
Step 3:
Pound until it becomes smooth with a dough like consistency. Wrap immediately and keep aside.
Step 4:
Serve with your favorite soup or stew. Served mine with egusi soup.

Jollof Rice

The popular jollof rice is a classic recipe that makes it to almost all special occasions in Nigeria. It is a rich, tasty, and elegant meal consisting of rice cooked with pepper, broth, seasoning, and spices in one pot. Serve this amazing meal with any protein of your choice.

Recipe

  1. Rice
  2. Canned tomato, fresh tomatoes, bell peppers, scotch bonnet, onions, garlic, and ginger
  3. Vegetable oil
  4. Stock—Chicken, beef, or vegetable stock are great choices here for adding depth of flavor.
  5. seasoning and herbs—curry powder, salt, black/white pepper, bouillon powder, thyme, and bay leaves.
  6. Meat and meat stock
  7. Palm oil, seasoning cube and salt

Procedure

Step 1:
The inevitable foundation for building up the flavor in this meal is to start by sauteeing the Onions. This should take about 3 to 5 minutes. The next thing is to stir in the tomato paste. This adds a deep and rich tomato flavor; I simply fry this for another 5 minutes or thereabout.
Step 2:
Add the ginger and garlic and cook along with the tomato paste for another two minutes.
Step 3:
Add the blended peppers.
Step 4:
Add the thyme, curry powder, salt, white pepper (good but optional), and seasoning cubes. Adjust the seasoning at this point if there is a need to.
Step 5:
Add the chicken stock.
Step 6:
Once the rice comes to a boil, reduce the heat to medium-low immediately and continue to cook until the rice is done, about 20 to 30 minutes. Serve with Fried Chicken, Spicy Grilled Chicken, Grilled Tilapia Fish, or Gizzards and plantains.
